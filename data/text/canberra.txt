Was built as a B.6 and issued to 9 Squadron on 31st October, 1955 who was based at RAF Conningsby where it served for six years. In 1956, the aircraft was flown to Cyprus in support of ‘Operation Musketeer’, the Suez Crisis, but it is not known whether it dropped any live munitions.  On 15th July, 1961 the aircraft was transferred back to English Electric and converted to B.15 specification and was used as a trials aircraft by the company and the A&AEE. Struck off charge on 28th September, 1972, WT205 was broken up but fortunately the nose section was saved and arrived at the museum on 14th October, 2000.
